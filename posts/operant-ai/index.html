Executive Summary

The problem: PMs spend 60% of their time on delivery logistics — translating requirements into specs, babysitting implementation, QA-ing output, and re-explaining context after every handoff. That’s 15–25 hours per week not spent on product thinking.

The product: Operant AI replaces that overhead with a phone call. You describe what you want built, hang up, and a deterministic pipeline — powered by AI agents but governed by an 18-state hardcoded state machine — writes specs, builds code, visually audits the result, and schedules a Google Meet walkthrough. The loop continues until you say “done.”

Traction: $10K revenue in 2 months across 4 paying companies (including a $60K first pilot). 900+ member PM community across NYC, Boston, and SF. Three Australian founders — ex-AWS, ex-SIG, ex-Telstra — with prior exits and featured open-source work.

The thesis: Other tools make PMs faster at delivery. We remove them from delivery entirely. This isn’t SaaS that assists — it’s SaiS that operates.

07 / DEFENSIBILITY

This is not a GPT wrapper

The product’s value comes from deep non-AI infrastructure that the AI operates within — not from a thin prompt layer on top of a model API.

Moat 1 — The Product Layer

A hardcoded state machine with 18 states and 23 events

Phase transitions are deterministic TypeScript — not LLM decisions. Strip the AI out entirely and you still have a full delivery pipeline: file-based state, multi-channel gates (voice + WhatsApp with timeout escalation), visual audit loops, revision stacking, and crash-resumable architecture. The pipeline is as reliable as CI/CD. The AI does the creative work inside each phase, but the process enforcement is pure product engineering.

Moat 2 — Product UX

The phone is the interface — and that’s the moat

Every incumbent is constrained by their existing product design: IDEs, chat windows, ticket boards. They cannot retrofit “call in, hang up, get a Google Meet demo” onto their architecture. Operant is AI-native from the ground up — built for a world where the PM never touches a keyboard. This is the Leonardo vs. Midjourney bet, taken further.

Moat 3 — Orchestration

Multi-agent coordination is an engineering problem, not a prompting problem

The pipeline coordinates spec-writing agents, 3-agent dev teams (Maintainer → Builder → Police), and visual audit agents — each with scoped skills, constraints, and handoff protocols. This orchestration layer compounds: every pipeline run generates data on blocker patterns, revision frequencies, and agent performance that tightens the system.

The testIf the underlying models improve 10x tomorrow, Operant gets better — it doesn’t get displaced. The pipeline is the product. The AI is the workforce.

11 / WHY NOW

The window is open

  • Models crossed the capability threshold. Claude Opus and GPT-4o can reliably write production code, understand specs, and follow multi-step instructions. This wasn’t true 18 months ago.
  • Voice AI is production-ready. Retell, ElevenLabs, and Twilio’s voice APIs make phone-based interfaces viable at startup scale and cost.
  • SaaS → SaiS creates category openings. Incumbents (Jira, Linear, Notion) are bolting AI features onto existing architectures. They cannot rebuild around a phone-first, agent-operated paradigm. The window for AI-native entrants is now.
  • PMs are ready. The “vibe coding” wave proved PMs will use AI to ship. But they don’t want to learn Cursor — they want to stay in their lane. Operant meets them where they are: on a phone call.
